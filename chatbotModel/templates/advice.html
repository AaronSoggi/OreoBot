Student advice

Supporting the mental health of students from across the UK is an imperative requirement which needs an effective response across higher education so that students can get the support they need in times of struggle.

In addition to chatbot applications such as OreoBot, there are numerous resources that students can access, to help eradicate any negative thoughts they experience.

Where can I get support?



Talk to another individual

Talking about your issues to another person can always develop a positive mind set and make you feel better about yourself.

People that you could talk to:


  • Teachers
  • Student support services
  • Family/friends
  • Therapists
  • Doctors

    • Student support services or tutors at your university can direct you towards many useful therapy services. Below are some mental health crisis helplines:


  • Samaritans - You can call 116 123, free from any phone or you can contact the samaritans welsh language line on 0808 164 0123. The samaritans are open from 7pm-11pm everyday.
  • The Mix - The mix work with people who are under 25 only. They are available from Sunday -Friday 2pm -11pm and can You can contacted on 0808 808 4994
  • SANEline - if you or any of your friends are experiencing a mental health condition you can contact SANEline on 0300 304 7000. They are open from 4:30pm-10:30pm everyday.
  • Campaign Agaisnt Living Miserably(CALM) - Calm is for males only, and are open from 5pm - midnight everyday. They can be contacted on 0800 58 58 58.


    • University student support

    Universities can be very helpful towards people with mental health conditions. They usually have a qualified therapist to help you access the support you need.

    In addition to this, there are many other ways they can contribute towards eliminating barriers that are making it hard for students to study. Examples include:



  • Arranging a structural approach towards your studies that would be most convenient for you.
  • Making adjustments to your exams and coursework, which may involve extensions and more time to complete exams.
  • Communicating with student finances about funding issues.


    • Online help

    There are websites which you can explore:


  • Mental health and wellbeing information
  • Students against depression website
  • Information and support on the mind.org website.

    • Therapy and counselling

    Therapy and counselling are two very useful methods which allow you to understand ways to cope with your feelings in a safe environment. Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) which is the most widely used talking therapy in the UK has proven to help manage issues by changing the way you think and behave.

    Disabled students allowance(DSA)

    At all UK universities students are given the opportunity to have one to one mentoring with a specialist, through Disabled Students Allowance (DSA)

    The DSA can provide funds for the following:


  • Facilities such as PCs and equipment if the student is in need of one due to mental health conditions or a disability
  • Travel funds so that your able to attend your lessons or work placement.
  • Assistants such as a sign language interpreter or specialist note taker.
  • Other support associated with studying such as printing numerous copies of documentation for proof reading.

    • If you decide to not apply for DSA, you will still be given advice so that you know what other options are available.
